turnarounds

How to spot them

  • it is safer to back blue-chips
  • a company is not necessarily in trouble if its sales are falling. If it is curring costs faster than receipts are falling, its financial situation could be improving
  • improving gross margins is one of the best ways to spot a turnaround candidate
  • sales and admin costs should also be falling. If not, it means management has not done any necessary belt-tightening
  • rising cash
Source: Investing; how to spot the turnaround stocks. August 1983
